Symbiotic Relationships

An example of commensalism in the Arctic Tundra is the arctic fox and the caribou. The caribou feed on lichens and when they are looking for food, the arctic fox follows.

Examples of mutualism in the tundra involves lichen. Lichen represents a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and an algae. The fungus recieves sugars from the photosynthetic alga and the alga receives protection from the fungus.

Examples of parasitism in the tundra involves ticks and caribou. Ticks feed off of the blood of caribou, and many other animals, as well. In this scenario, only the tick is benefitted from the relationship.
