Three Animal Adaptations

The Tundra is a biome where lots of animals donâ€™t stay and usually leave and migrate to somewhere warmer

Polar bear - their white fur makes them camouflage with the ice and keep them safe from the few enemies they have. Also they have a fat under their skin to help them stay warm from the freezing temperatures.

Reindeer/Caribou - they possess a coat that provides insulation against cold temperatures. They have a compact, stocky body with a short tail and ears to avoid losing body heat. Their skinny legs have veins and arteries that run side by side, so that the heat of the arterial blood coming from the body warms the cooler venous blood returning from the lower legs.

Gray wolf - They survive by eating other animals since they are carnivores. They also have a thick coat which helps with the cold as well as sense of smell 100x stronger then humans which can help them smell prey miles away.
